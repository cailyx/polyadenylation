In the cell, a freshly made strand of RNA is commonly extended further by successive rounds of adenines (A's) added by poly(A) polymerase to create a "poly(A) tail." Why, when, and how does tail growth stop? In other words, what sets the length of the tail, or how does the polymerase 'know' when to stop? Here we conceive of a particular polyA tail length as being defined by some critical point, a notion wanting of characterization...
